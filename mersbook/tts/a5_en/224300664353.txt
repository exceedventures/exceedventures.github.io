Title: A Great Place for a Saturday Walk in Yeouido

I often skip lunch and go for a walk instead.

For my lunchtime walks, I usually either make a full loop around Yeouido Park or take a route along Yunjung-ro, the road that circles the National Assembly grounds.

Before the cherry blossom festival begins, Yunjung-ro is a quiet and pleasant place to walk.

People who know the area well sometimes make a small detour from the usual Yunjung-ro route.

Can you guess where the photo above was taken?

It’s the National Assembly.

Inside the National Assembly grounds, there is a small wooded area where people can sit together and enjoy their packed lunches during the lunch hour.

There is also a retro-style pond.

There are forest trails where you can burn some calories, as well as benches and observation spots where you can enjoy both the cherry blossoms and the Han River while sipping a takeaway coffee.

Personally, I love the view of the Han River and the Yunjung-ro cherry blossoms from this spot.

There are security guards standing at the entrance to the National Assembly grounds.

Sometimes, when you try to enter, they ask, “Where are you going?” But if you answer, “I’m just taking a walk,” they usually let you in.

Until September of last year, I had a favorite restaurant on the sixth floor of the National Assembly Members’ Office Building.

It was an Italian restaurant called Cucina Hu.

The prices were reasonable, and because it was located inside the National Assembly—a place most people rarely visit—it was always well received when I invited guests there.

After the restaurant closed, the space appears to have been converted into a meeting room for official visitors.

Seeing how freely ordinary citizens used to come and go through the building, I sometimes think that some of the important people working there may have found it inconvenient.

A better place has appeared, though.

If you enter through the main gate of the National Assembly and head up the hill on the right, you’ll find a traditional Korean hanok building called Sarangjae.

Following the wooden deck path on the right side of Sarangjae leads to an outdoor garden.

There you’ll find a book-and-bakery café called Gangbyeon Seojae.

The space is open to the public. Visitors can browse the books available there and order drinks and pastries.

An Americano costs 4,000 won.

The Han River view through the large floor-to-ceiling windows is wonderful, but the rooftop view is even more refreshing.

The scenery is particularly interesting. On one side, you can see the Han River; in the distance, the forest of office towers in Yeouido; and closer by, the elegant eaves of the traditional hanok building.

Taking an iced Americano to go and enjoying it while looking out over the river creates a surprisingly pleasant atmosphere.

Because Gangbyeon Seojae was built on elevated pillars, it offers an unobstructed view of the Han River above the treetops of Yunjung-ro.

Driving there can be a bit inconvenient.

If you happen to find a parking space along Yunjung-ro, you can park there. Otherwise, you’ll need to walk about 200 meters to the riverside parking lot near the National Assembly.

It’s also not a bad option to take public transportation, as it’s about 900 meters from Exit 1 of National Assembly Station.

On public holidays like today, however, parking is allowed inside the National Assembly grounds.

If someone at the entrance asks why you’re visiting, you can simply say you’re attending a wedding.

One-line comment

When I’m walking alone on weekdays, I usually stroll through Yeouido Park. On weekends, when I’m with my wife, I seem to find myself walking along Yunjung-ro instead.
