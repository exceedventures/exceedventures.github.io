One-line comment.
On May 20, 2026, Geumyang was delisted and removed from the Korean stock market. The company has filed for an injunction against the delisting decision, so liquidation trading will proceed only after the court ruling. Geumyang’s main asset is its Gijang factory, but foreclosure proceedings are already underway by Busan Bank, which provided the loans. Even if liquidation trading proceeds, it is unclear whether shareholders — who rank last in debt repayment priority — will receive anything at all. A stock that once traded at 2,000 won briefly soared to 200,000 won. The Korean stock market is truly a dynamic place.
