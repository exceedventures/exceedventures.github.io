There is a company that has been mentioned three times since it was first brought up in June 2024.
That company is FADU.

Each time I posted about it, the tone became slightly less intense, but it was a company that consistently received many negative comments.

The most recent post was written on February 3, 2026, titled “Why Was FADU Suspended from Trading? Update.”
It was an update to the December 19 post titled “Why Was FADU Suspended from Trading?”, written after the company was suspended in December 2025.

At the time of the suspension in December 2025, the stock price was 21,250 KRW.

To understand what happened to FADU, it would be helpful to refer to the post written in December 2025.
(It runs from sections 1 to 61, so you may skip it if you remember.)

On December 19, 2025, the exchange announced that it had confirmed that FADU had either falsely recorded or omitted important information during the listing review process.
It also stated that it would determine whether this constituted a significant reason to subject the company to a substantive listing eligibility review.

A substantive listing review is a process in which the exchange decides whether a company can remain listed when serious issues arise.
Before initiating this review, the exchange suspends trading and first determines whether the company should be subject to such a review.

The decision must be made within 15 business days (by January 13, 2026) from the date of occurrence (December 19), with a possible one-time extension.

If the exchange determines that the company does not need a substantive review, trading resumes.
If a review is required, the company may either be delisted or given a period for improvement after deliberation.

FADU was suspended, and the decision process was underway.

FADU is a Korean semiconductor design company. Since it does not have manufacturing facilities, it is classified as a fabless company.

Controversy arose because the difference between the projected and actual revenue in its listing documents was too large, leading to allegations of fraudulent listing.

The company had projected annual revenue of 120.2 billion KRW, but the actual figure was only 22.4 billion KRW.

The real issue was not the annual revenue, but the quarterly revenue.
Immediately after listing, the company reported quarterly revenue of only about 50 million KRW.

This sparked controversy, as a company valued at 1.5 trillion KRW recorded such low quarterly revenue.

FADU’s main product is an SSD controller.
An SSD consists of multiple NAND flash memory units, which retain data even without power.

However, NAND is relatively slow and vulnerable to heat, so connecting many units can cause performance and durability issues.
The SSD controller was developed to address these problems.

It controls multiple NAND units to improve data processing speed and durability, which is why it is often referred to as the “brain of the SSD.”

FADU’s clients include SK hynix and Meta (Facebook).
It supplies Gen 3 SSD controllers to SK hynix and Gen 4 SSD products to Meta.

FADU passed Meta’s SSD controller certification, and Meta requested SK hynix to supply data center SSDs equipped with FADU controllers.

This business structure began operating in late 2021.

FADU’s revenue grew from 5.1 billion KRW in 2021 to 56.4 billion KRW in 2022, with 78% coming from SSD controllers.

The company was listed on KOSDAQ on August 7, 2023.

In its listing report, FADU estimated 2023 revenue at 120.2 billion KRW and operating profit at 100 million KRW.

However, on November 9, 2023, previously undisclosed Q2 and Q3 results were revealed:
59 million KRW in Q2 and 300 million KRW in Q3.

The total for both quarters was only 359 million KRW, causing the stock price to plunge and triggering a major market issue.

The reason for the revenue drop was that both Meta and SK hynix halted orders.

As FADU had only two major clients, revenue disappeared when both stopped ordering.

FADU relies on TSMC in Taiwan for manufacturing its advanced controllers.
Since TSMC selectively accepts clients, FADU’s production requests were likely to be deprioritized.

SK hynix transferred part of its TSMC production allocation to FADU, equivalent to 1.2 million SSD controllers.

FADU reportedly forecast demand based on this expected supply.

In 2023, the semiconductor industry was in a downturn. Even Samsung Electronics recorded losses.

The key issue is whether FADU failed to disclose the risk of order suspension in its listing report despite anticipating it.

FADU’s technology itself is not under dispute.
Its core researchers are highly experienced experts from Seoul National University.

The SSD market is rapidly growing due to AI data center demand.
FADU is one of the few companies capable of independently designing SSD controllers for AI data centers.

Although revenue dropped significantly in 2023, it recovered to 43.5 billion KRW in 2024 and continued to grow in 2025.

In 2026, the company achieved its first-ever quarterly profit, with revenue of 59.5 billion KRW and operating profit of 7.7 billion KRW in Q1.

The Korean exchange allowed trading to resume after determining that delisting was not necessary.

As of today, FADU closed at 81,300 KRW, marking a 654% increase over the past year.

One-line comment:
Investing based on logic rather than emotion tends to yield better results.
